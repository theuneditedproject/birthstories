# Birth Stories Project. Plan

A collection of raw, unfiltered birth stories from mothers around the world.

## The vision

A book and digital gallery where women tell their birth stories in their own words. Joyful, traumatic, complicated, ordinary. All of it. No sanitizing. The kind of stories women wish they'd read before going through it themselves.

**Title:** *Unedited. Birth stories in their own words*

---

## Phase 1. Foundation (weeks 1–4)

Goal: get the digital gallery live with your story and 2–3 others.

- [ ] Set up project email and GitHub account separate from your real identity
- [ ] Sign up for Web3Forms (free, no credit card), get the access key, paste it into `index.html`
- [ ] Push the repo to GitHub, enable Pages
- [ ] Buy a domain (~$12/yr at Namecheap or Porkbun). Optional but worth it
- [ ] Polish your own story. Let the draft sit, revise once
- [ ] Personally invite 3–5 women you know to submit early stories
- [ ] Update the About page text with your specific framing
- [ ] Decide your moderation philosophy before submissions arrive

## Phase 2. Growth (months 2–6)

Goal: build to ~50–80 stories.

- [ ] Share on relevant communities: r/BabyBumps, r/Mommit, r/beyondthebump, BabyCenter, postpartum support groups, doula networks, midwifery associations
- [ ] Reach out to birth photographers and doulas. They know women with stories to tell
- [ ] Translate the submission page into 3–5 languages: Spanish, Portuguese, French, Arabic, Mandarin
- [ ] Set up a newsletter (Buttondown or Substack)
- [ ] Start an Instagram or TikTok with story excerpts (with permission). Drives most submissions
- [ ] Track every submission as an issue in your private tracking repo

## Phase 3. The book (months 6–18)

Goal: turn the collection into a publishable book.

- [ ] Read all stories, identify themes. Themes become chapters
- [ ] Decide on structure (probably thematic)
- [ ] Light editing only. Preserve voice. Fix typos, clarify, never "improve."
- [ ] Get separate written consent for every story going in the book
- [ ] Pick publication path:
  - Self-publish (KDP, IngramSpark). Full control, you keep rights, ~$0 upfront
  - Small press. Better distribution, less control, takes 1–2 years
  - Traditional publisher. Needs a literary agent, longest path, biggest reach
- [ ] Hire a copyeditor (~$1,500–3,000)
- [ ] Don't skimp on cover design (Reedsy, 99designs)
- [ ] Author credit: "Edited by [Pen Name]" or "Compiled by [Project Name]"

---

## Things to think about

- **Heavy stories take a toll.** You'll be reading lots of birth trauma, including stories that may resonate with your own. Build in breaks. Find 1–2 co-readers you trust.
- **Moderation policy.** What do you do with stories that include misinformation (anti-vax, unsafe practices)? Probably publish with a small editor's note. Decide before it comes up.
- **Names of doctors, hospitals, partners.** Many submitters will name them. Default policy: remove identifying details unless the submitter explicitly insists.
- **Your own story is the seed.** Don't overthink it. Publish as-is, revise later. The "I wish I could retell this story in a more joyful way" line is exactly the tone that will draw other women in.
